When you read MIL-STD-130 and DFARS 252.211-7003, the complexity of the regulations stands out. Terms include UII (unique item identifier), UID (unique identification) and IUID (item unique identification). We explain the DoD requirements simply and comprehensively so you can focus on your business. We guide your selection of the key elements of the UID label:

  • Data Construct - to match the way you serialize

    Choices: Construct #1 or Construct #2

  • Data Format - to meet DoD requirements

    Choices: Data Identifier, Text Element Identifier or Application Identifier

  • Material - to meet field conditions, specifications and your budget

    Choices: Stainless Steel, Photo Anodized Aluminum, tesa® Secure™, Polyimide, Polyester and Black Anodized Aluminum (see table below)

  • Fastening method - to match your substrate and environment

    Choices: 'Peel & Stick' adhesive or mechanical fasteners
